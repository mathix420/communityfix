# How to Structure an Issue on CommunityFix

## Step 1: Identify the core problem

Ask yourself: **if someone on the other side of the world had this same problem, what would they call it?**

Strip away your location, your specific causes, your local context. What remains is your **issue title**.

> **Example:** "Deforestation" — not "Deforestation in the Amazon Rainforest"

## Step 2: Break it into causes

Ask: **what are the distinct root causes or dimensions of this problem?**

Each one becomes a **sub-issue**. They should be independent enough that someone might experience one without the others, but related enough to belong under the same parent.

> **Example** under "Deforestation":
>
> - Illegal Logging
> - Agricultural Land Expansion
> - Wildfire Acceleration

## Step 3: Localize it

Ask: **where specifically am I experiencing this?**

Your region becomes a **sub-issue under the relevant cause(s)**. This is where you put all the local detail: specific conditions, severity, affected areas, local actors involved, history.

> **Example:** "Amazon Basin, Brazil" under "Agricultural Land Expansion"

A single cause can have many regional entries. "Illegal Logging" might have sub-issues for the Amazon, Borneo, and the Congo Basin — each with their own local reality.

## Step 4: Write the content at each level

- **Issue**: broad description of the global problem, why it matters, what's at stake.
- **Cause sub-issue**: explain the mechanism, how this cause works generally, why it's hard to fix.
- **Regional sub-issue**: your local context, specific data, who's affected, what's been tried, what help is needed.

## Step 5: Solutions come separately

Don't embed solutions in the issue. Solutions are their own entities that attach to whichever level they apply to:

- A solution for one region? Attach it to that regional sub-issue.
- A solution for the cause globally? Attach it to the cause sub-issue.
- A solution for the whole problem? Attach it to the parent issue.

Each solution acts as a community and R&D space until it's applied and produces results.

---

## Quick checklist

- [ ] Is my issue title region-free and globally recognizable?
- [ ] Are my sub-issues distinct causes, not duplicates?
- [ ] Is my regional context at the deepest level, not in the title?
- [ ] Are solutions separate from the problem description?
