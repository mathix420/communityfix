Subject: Ahmedabad Heat Action Plan featured on CommunityFix — review & enrich

Dear Ms. Bagai and the NRDC India team,

My name is Arnaud Gissinger, and I am the founder of CommunityFix (communityfix.org), an open platform that documents real-world solutions to societal challenges so that practitioners around the world can learn from and replicate them.

Ahmedabad's Heat Action Plan — developed together with Ahmedabad Municipal Corporation, IIPH Gandhinagar, and the Public Health Foundation of India — is one of the most cited examples of a heat-early-warning system that has demonstrably saved lives (estimates point to roughly 1,100 lives saved per year). We have documented it as a reference case study on CommunityFix, where it serves as a model for city governments worldwide tackling extreme heat.

I am reaching out for two reasons:

1. **Accuracy review** — We want to make sure every detail in the case study reflects what actually happened on the ground. If anything is incomplete or imprecise, we would very much welcome your corrections.

2. **Enrichment** — If NRDC India is willing to share additional context — lessons learned, implementation challenges, updated metrics, links to reports or evaluations — we can incorporate them directly into the record, giving your work even greater reach and replicability.

This would cost very little of your team's time; even a brief email with notes would be enormously helpful. There is no commercial angle — CommunityFix is a public-interest platform.

The idea behind CommunityFix is simple: by documenting what was actually tried — and what came of it, costs and pitfalls included — other cities, regions and countries can avoid the same mistakes and go straight to the solutions that work. It's a small but real contribution to a better world: turning one community's hard-won experience into knowledge everyone can build on.

Thank you for the pioneering work NRDC India and its partners have done on urban heat resilience. I hope we can collaborate to make this knowledge available to as many cities as possible.

Warm regards,

Arnaud Gissinger
