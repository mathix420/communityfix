Hi Lachlan (and Dilara),

I'm Arnaud, founder of CommunityFix (communityfix.org) — a public knowledge base that documents real-world attempts to solve urban and environmental challenges, including projects that are still ongoing or inconclusive.

I came across TerraCool — your open-source ceramic evaporative cooling modules developed at the Bartlett — and I thought it was exactly the kind of project we want on the platform. The fact that it uses passive, low-tech fabrication logic to address heat stress in dense urban environments is genuinely valuable to document, regardless of where the outcome currently sits.

I've added a case study entry for TerraCool (case study #64). Before it goes fully public I'd love for you both to:

1. **Check it for accuracy** — the programme description, your roles, the outcome framing.
2. **Enrich it** if you're happy to — metrics, key lessons learned, links to documentation or fabrication files, or anything you think would help someone trying to replicate or build on the work.

You can view and edit the case study directly at: https://communityfix.org/case-studies/64

The idea behind CommunityFix is simple: by documenting what was actually tried — and what came of it, costs and pitfalls included — other cities, regions and countries can avoid the same mistakes and go straight to the solutions that work. It's a small but real contribution to a better world: turning one community's hard-won experience into knowledge everyone can build on.

There's no obligation here — even a quick "looks fine" reply is helpful.

Thanks for building something worth documenting.

Arnaud Gissinger
