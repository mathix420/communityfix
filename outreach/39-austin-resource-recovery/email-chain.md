**Subject:** Your chicken-keeping programme is featured on CommunityFix

Hi,

I hope this finds you well. My name is Arnaud Gissinger — I'm the founder of CommunityFix (communityfix.org), an open platform that documents real-world solutions to urban and environmental challenges so communities everywhere can learn from them.

Austin Resource Recovery's backyard chicken programme caught our attention as a particularly thoughtful model: pairing a free chicken-keeping class with a $75 coop-construction rebate lowers barriers for residents without the liability and logistics of distributing live hens. It's exactly the kind of nuanced, replicable design that other cities need to see.

We've published it as a reference case study on CommunityFix. The idea behind the platform is simple: by documenting what was actually tried — and what came of it, costs and pitfalls included — other cities, regions and countries can avoid the same mistakes and go straight to the solutions that work. It's a small but real contribution to a better world: turning one community's hard-won experience into knowledge everyone can build on.

I'm reaching out for two reasons:

1. **Accuracy** — we'd love you to take a quick look and flag anything we've got wrong or that has changed.
2. **Enrichment** — if there's additional data you're able to share (participation numbers, composting diversion figures, programme updates), it would make the case study even more useful to practitioners in other cities considering a similar approach.

The case study is live at: https://communityfix.org

There's no cost or obligation — we just want the record to be as accurate and useful as possible.

Thank you for the work you do. I'd be happy to answer any questions about the platform.
