## Message 1 — Initial outreach

**To:** milieudienst@opwijk.be  
**Subject:** Your free-hen programme is featured on CommunityFix

---

Dear Opwijk Environment Department,

I hope this message finds you well.

My name is Arnaud Gissinger, and I'm the founder of CommunityFix (communityfix.org) — an open platform that documents real-world solutions to community and environmental challenges so others can learn from them and replicate what works.

We recently added a case study describing Opwijk and INZET's initiative to distribute free laying hens to 30 households — 60 hens in total — through the municipality's annual garden market. It's a beautifully simple programme: low overhead, direct environmental benefit (food-waste reduction through home composting), and a natural fit alongside an event the community already attends.

The idea behind CommunityFix is simple: by documenting what was actually tried — and what came of it, costs and pitfalls included — other cities, regions and countries can avoid the same mistakes and go straight to the solutions that work. It's a small but real contribution to a better world: turning one community's hard-won experience into knowledge everyone can build on.

I'm reaching out for two reasons:

1. **Accuracy check** — We want the record to reflect the programme as you know it. If any detail is off, we'd be glad to correct it.
2. **Enrichment** — If you're open to sharing a bit more — metrics (waste diverted, household feedback, repeat editions), lessons learned, or photos — that context makes the case study much more useful to other municipalities considering a similar scheme.

You can view the case study at: https://communityfix.org

There's no obligation at all. Even a quick "looks right to us" would be helpful.

Thank you for the work you're doing, and for taking a moment to read this.

Warm regards,  
Arnaud Gissinger  
Founder — CommunityFix · communityfix.org
