## Message 1 — Initial outreach

**To:** milieu@diest.be
**Subject:** Diest's hen-distribution scheme on CommunityFix — review invited

---

Dear Milieu en Groen team,

I hope this message finds you well.

I am writing on behalf of CommunityFix (communityfix.org), an open platform that documents real-world solutions to community and environmental challenges. We recently published a case study on Diest's household hen-distribution programme — one of the earliest and largest schemes of its kind in Europe, which reached around 2,000 families and placed roughly 6,000 hens across the city in the mid-2000s.

We believe it is an important reference for other municipalities considering similar initiatives, and we want to make sure the record is accurate and as useful as possible.

The idea behind CommunityFix is simple: by documenting what was actually tried — and what came of it, costs and pitfalls included — other cities, regions and countries can avoid the same mistakes and go straight to the solutions that work. It's a small but real contribution to a better world: turning one community's hard-won experience into knowledge everyone can build on.

We would be grateful if someone from your team could:
1. Take a few minutes to review the case study for factual accuracy.
2. Share any additional detail you are able to — final participation figures, outcomes, lessons learned, or links to reports.

You can view the case study here: https://communityfix.org

There is no obligation and the review can be as brief as a quick read-through. If anything is wrong or missing, please simply reply to this email and we will update the record.

Thank you for the pioneering work Diest did with this programme.

Kind regards,

Arnaud Gissinger
Founder — CommunityFix · communityfix.org
