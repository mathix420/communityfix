## Message 1 — Initial outreach

**To:** assemblymember.kalra@assembly.ca.gov
**Subject:** AB 1162 documented on CommunityFix — invitation to review

Dear Assemblymember Kalra,

My name is Arnaud Gissinger, and I am the founder of CommunityFix (communityfix.org), an open platform that maps real-world solutions to shared civic and environmental problems so that policymakers, advocates, and communities anywhere can replicate what works.

I am writing to let you know that California AB 1162 — which prohibits hotels from providing small single-use plastic toiletry bottles — is documented on CommunityFix as a reference case study. The legislation you authored stands out as a concrete, replicable model for reducing plastic waste at scale, and we believe many other jurisdictions would benefit from learning from California's approach.

The idea behind CommunityFix is simple: by documenting what was actually tried — and what came of it, costs and pitfalls included — other cities, regions and countries can avoid the same mistakes and go straight to the solutions that work. It's a small but real contribution to a better world: turning one community's hard-won experience into knowledge everyone can build on.

I would like to invite you (or a member of your team) to:

1. **Review the entry for accuracy** — confirm that the facts, timeline, and outcome we have recorded reflect the actual implementation.
2. **Enrich it with recent information** — any updated figures, implementation lessons, or resources you are comfortable sharing would make the case study far more useful to others seeking to replicate it.

If it is easier to point me to a staff contact who handles policy documentation, I am happy to follow up with them directly.

Thank you for your leadership on this issue. I hope CommunityFix can help AB 1162 inspire similar action beyond California.

Warm regards,

Arnaud Gissinger
Founder — CommunityFix · communityfix.org
