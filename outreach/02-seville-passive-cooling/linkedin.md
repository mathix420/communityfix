## Post-connection message

Dear colleagues at the Ayuntamiento de Sevilla,

Thank you for connecting. I wanted to share why I reached out.

I run CommunityFix (communityfix.org), an open platform that documents real-world solutions to urban challenges — heat, flooding, air quality — so other cities and communities can replicate them.

We have published a reference case study on two of Seville's landmark climate initiatives:

- The **CartujaQanat** project, which revived a ~3,000-year-old underground passive-cooling system to reduce urban heat in public spaces.
- Seville's pioneering work as the **first city to formally name and categorize heatwaves**, raising public awareness and enabling better emergency response.

Both are outstanding examples of adaptive urban governance, and we believe they deserve to be known and replicated far beyond Spain.

We would be very grateful if someone from the Gerencia de Urbanismo y Medio Ambiente — or from the research team at the University of Seville — could:

1. **Review the case study** for accuracy (a few minutes of your time).
2. **Add recent figures or lessons learned** that would help other cities implement similar approaches.

There is no commercial purpose; CommunityFix is a public-interest resource. By documenting what was actually tried — costs and pitfalls included — other cities can avoid the same mistakes and go straight to what works, turning one community's hard-won experience into knowledge everyone can build on.

If this reaches the right person, please feel free to reply here or contact us through communityfix.org.

Thank you for the remarkable work your city is doing on urban heat resilience.
