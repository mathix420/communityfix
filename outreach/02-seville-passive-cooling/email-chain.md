## Message 1 — Initial outreach

**To:** sostenibilidadeinnovacion@urbanismo-sevilla.org
**Subject:** CommunityFix — Seville's CartujaQanat documented as a reference case study

Dear Professor Sánchez Ramos,

My name is Arnaud Gissinger and I am the founder of CommunityFix (communityfix.org), an open platform that documents real-world solutions to urban and environmental challenges so that other cities and communities can replicate them.

I am writing to let you know that the work carried out by the City of Seville — reviving the CartujaQanat underground passive-cooling system and pioneering a formal heatwave naming and categorization framework — has been documented as a reference case study on CommunityFix. Given Seville's position as one of Europe's most heat-exposed cities, this programme represents exactly the kind of transferable, evidence-based approach that other municipalities urgently need.

The idea behind CommunityFix is simple: by documenting what was actually tried — and what came of it, costs and pitfalls included — other cities, regions and countries can avoid the same mistakes and go straight to the solutions that work. It's a small but real contribution to a better world: turning one community's hard-won experience into knowledge everyone can build on.

I would be very grateful if you and your colleagues at the Gerencia de Urbanismo y Medio Ambiente could take a few minutes to:

1. **Review the case study for accuracy** — please flag any details that need correcting.
2. **Enrich it with recent data** — updated figures, lessons learned, or photographs would make the record far more useful to cities looking to replicate the approach.

There is no commercial angle here; CommunityFix is a public-interest resource and all content is freely accessible.

Would you be open to a short call or email exchange to go through the case study together?

Thank you very much for your time and for the pioneering work your team has done.

Warm regards,

Arnaud Gissinger
Founder — CommunityFix · communityfix.org
