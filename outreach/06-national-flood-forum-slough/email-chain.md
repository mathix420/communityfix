I hope this finds you well. I'm Arnaud Gissinger, founder of CommunityFix (communityfix.org) — an open platform where communities can discover, document, and replicate solutions to local challenges.

We've published a case study on Project Sponge, covering the Flood Action Groups in Chalvey and HCB, Slough — the work your team has been doing to restore streams, install rain gardens, and sustain meaningful public engagement around flood resilience.

I'm writing for two reasons:

1. **Accuracy review** — We want to make sure the case study reflects the work correctly. If anything is imprecise or out of date, we'd be grateful for a correction.
2. **Enrichment** — If you're open to it, any recent figures, lessons learned, or photos you could share would make the case study significantly more useful for other communities looking to replicate similar flood action work.

The goal is not publicity for its own sake, but to give practitioners in other flood-prone areas a solid, honest reference they can actually learn from. The idea behind CommunityFix is simple: by documenting what was actually tried — and what came of it, costs and pitfalls included — other cities, regions and countries can avoid the same mistakes and go straight to the solutions that work. It's a small but real contribution to a better world: turning one community's hard-won experience into knowledge everyone can build on.

I'd be happy to share the current draft with you if that would be helpful.

With many thanks for the important work the National Flood Forum does,
