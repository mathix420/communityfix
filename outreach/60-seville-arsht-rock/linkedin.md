## Follow-up message (after connection accepted)

Hi,

Thanks for connecting. I wanted to share a bit more context on why I reached out.

CommunityFix (communityfix.org) is an open knowledge base that helps cities and organisations find and replicate proven climate resilience solutions. We document real-world implementations — who did it, how, what happened — so practitioners elsewhere can learn from them directly. The idea behind it is simple: by documenting what was actually tried — and what came of it, costs and pitfalls included — other cities, regions and countries can avoid the same mistakes and go straight to the solutions that work. It's a small but real contribution to a better world: turning one community's hard-won experience into knowledge everyone can build on.

We've published a case study on the proMETEO pilot in Seville: the heat wave naming and categorisation system that gave the world its first named heat wave, "Zoe," developed with Arsht-Rock, the City of Seville, AEMET, and the local universities.

I'd love two things from your side if you're open to it:

1. A quick accuracy review — to make sure the case study reflects what actually happened and is up to date.
2. Any materials, metrics, or lessons you'd like to see included (linked or quoted, always attributed).

It's a public resource with no commercial angle. The aim is simply to make the proMETEO approach as replicable as possible for other cities facing extreme heat.

Would anyone on the Arsht-Rock team be the right person to loop in for this?
