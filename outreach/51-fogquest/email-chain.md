## Message 1 — Initial outreach

**To:** info@fogquest.org
**Subject:** El Tofo / Chungungo documented on CommunityFix — your review invited

---

Dear Dr. Schemenauer,

My name is Arnaud Gissinger and I am the founder of CommunityFix (communityfix.org), an open knowledge platform where communities and researchers document real-world solutions to local problems — what worked, what did not, and why.

I am writing because we have published a reference case study on the El Tofo fog-collection project at Chungungo, Coquimbo Region, Chile. The project holds a singular place in the fog-harvesting literature: the Standard Fog Collectors supplied a village with potable water for years, demonstrating that large-scale fog collection is technically viable. At the same time, the documented breakdown in community ownership and long-term maintenance makes it one of the most instructive failure cases in the field — precisely the kind of honest record that CommunityFix exists to preserve.

Because FogQuest grew directly from that experience, and because you have been involved with this work longer than almost anyone, I wanted to reach out in two ways:

1. **Accuracy review.** I would be grateful if you (or a colleague) could glance at the case study and flag anything that is factually incorrect or misleading. The URL is: https://communityfix.org (case study 51). Even a brief note by email would be enormously helpful.

2. **Enrichment.** If you are willing, we would welcome any additional detail you are able to share — metrics on peak daily water delivery, a more precise timeline of when maintenance responsibilities were handed over, or lessons FogQuest drew from the experience when designing subsequent projects. These additions would make the record far more useful to practitioners considering fog collection today.

The idea behind CommunityFix is simple: by documenting what was actually tried — and what came of it, costs and pitfalls included — other cities, regions and countries can avoid the same mistakes and go straight to the solutions that work. It's a small but real contribution to a better world: turning one community's hard-won experience into knowledge everyone can build on.

There is no obligation and no deadline. CommunityFix is a public good; the case study is already live and attributed to the research programme, not to any individual. I simply believe that a record this important deserves to be as accurate and complete as possible, and that you are better placed than anyone to help with that.

Thank you for your time, and for the decades of work that made Chungungo possible in the first place.

Warm regards,

Arnaud Gissinger
Founder — CommunityFix · communityfix.org
