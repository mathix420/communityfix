## Message 1 — Initial Outreach

**Subject:** Marriott's mini-bottle phase-out is a reference case study on CommunityFix

---

Dear Melissa,

I'm writing to let you know that Marriott's global elimination of single-use mini toiletry bottles — a programme that removed roughly 500 million plastic bottles a year from hotel rooms worldwide — is now documented as a reference case study on CommunityFix (communityfix.org).

CommunityFix is an open platform that maps real-world solutions to civic and sustainability challenges so that municipalities, businesses, and communities elsewhere can learn from and replicate what has already worked. Marriott's initiative stands out as one of the most concrete, scalable examples of hospitality-sector plastic reduction, and we want to make sure the record accurately reflects your team's work and results.

The idea behind CommunityFix is simple: by documenting what was actually tried — and what came of it, costs and pitfalls included — other cities, regions and countries can avoid the same mistakes and go straight to the solutions that work. It's a small but real contribution to a better world: turning one community's hard-won experience into knowledge everyone can build on.

I would love to invite you — or whoever on your team is closest to this programme — to:

1. **Review the case study for accuracy** — if any figures, dates, or framing need correcting, we will update them straight away.
2. **Enrich the record** — recent metrics, updated savings figures, lessons learned, or photos you are happy to share publicly would help others replicate this model.

The case study is live and publicly visible. I'm happy to share the direct link, walk you through the platform, or simply receive any corrections by email.

Thank you for the work Marriott has done on this — it genuinely gives other organisations a credible path to follow.

Warm regards,
