## Message 1 — Initial outreach

**Subject:** Singapore's City in Nature programme on CommunityFix — accuracy check

Dear NParks team,

I'm writing to let you know that Singapore's "City in Nature" initiative — including the mandatory greenery replacement requirement and the Skyrise Greenery scheme — is now documented as a reference case study on CommunityFix (communityfix.org), a free public platform that maps real-world solutions to urban and environmental challenges.

We highlighted the programme because it stands out as one of the most comprehensive urban greening frameworks globally: pairing a regulatory mandate (replacing ground-level greenery lost to development, storey for storey) with financial incentives through the Skyrise Greenery scheme to make rooftop and vertical gardens viable at scale.

We want to make sure the record is accurate and does your work justice. I'd be grateful if someone with knowledge of the programme could take a few minutes to review it and flag anything that needs correcting or expanding.

Case study link: https://communityfix.org (search "City in Nature Singapore")

If you're open to it, adding metrics, updated source links, or lessons learned directly on the platform would make it more useful to policymakers and urban planners looking to replicate Singapore's approach elsewhere.

Thank you for the remarkable work NParks has done on this — it genuinely sets a benchmark for cities worldwide.
