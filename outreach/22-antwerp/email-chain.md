# Email Chain — Case Study 22: City of Antwerp Free-Hen Programme

---

## Message 1 — Initial outreach

**To:** info@antwerpen.be
**Subject:** Your free-hen programme is featured on CommunityFix — can you check the details?

Dear Stad Antwerpen team,

I hope this note finds you well. My name is Arnaud Gissinger and I am the founder of CommunityFix (communityfix.org), an open knowledge platform that documents proven solutions to community challenges.

We have published a case study about Antwerp's free-hen food-waste programme — the initiative that distributed backyard hens to more than 1,000 households and measured a reduction of roughly 50 kg of organic waste per household per year. It is a concrete, well-evidenced example of how a city can turn a familiar animal into an effective waste-reduction tool, and we wanted to make sure it is represented accurately.

The idea behind CommunityFix is simple: by documenting what was actually tried — and what came of it, costs and pitfalls included — other cities, regions and countries can avoid the same mistakes and go straight to the solutions that work. It's a small but real contribution to a better world: turning one community's hard-won experience into knowledge everyone can build on.

We would love to ask you two things:

1. **Review the case study for accuracy** — if any details are off or have been updated, we are happy to correct them.
2. **Enrich it** — if there are newer figures, lessons learned, or materials you would like to add, we can incorporate them so other cities considering a similar programme have the best possible reference.

The case study is publicly accessible and free to use. We are simply trying to ensure the record is right and as useful as possible for practitioners elsewhere.

Would someone on the environmental team be the right person to look this over? I am happy to send a direct link and answer any questions.

Thank you for the work you do — this programme is a genuine model worth sharing.

Warm regards,

Arnaud Gissinger
Founder — CommunityFix · communityfix.org
