Dear Dr. Bargach,

I hope this message finds you well. My name is Arnaud Gissinger, and I'm the founder of CommunityFix (communityfix.org) — an open platform that maps real-world solutions to community challenges so that practitioners, researchers, and policymakers worldwide can learn from proven approaches.

I'm writing because we have documented Dar Si Hmad's fog-water harvesting programme on Mount Boutmezguida as a reference case study on CommunityFix. The system's scale — 600 villagers served, nets stretching across 6,300 m² of mountainside — and its model of centering women as operators and stewards make it one of the most instructive examples of community-led water access anywhere. We wanted the record to reflect the work accurately and with the depth it deserves.

The idea behind CommunityFix is simple: by documenting what was actually tried — and what came of it, costs and pitfalls included — other cities, regions and countries can avoid the same mistakes and go straight to the solutions that work. It's a small but real contribution to a better world: turning one community's hard-won experience into knowledge everyone can build on.

I have two small requests:

1. **Review for accuracy** — Would you or a colleague be willing to glance at the entry and flag anything we've got wrong or that needs updating?
2. **Enrich the record** — If there are metrics, lessons learned, or recent developments (e.g., expansions, replication efforts) you'd like added, we'd be glad to incorporate them. You're also welcome to link to Dar Si Hmad's own publications directly from the case study page.

There's no obligation, and it takes only a few minutes. The goal is simply to make sure the people who find this case study get an accurate picture of what your team has built.

Would you be open to a quick look?

Warm regards,
Arnaud Gissinger
