# Email sequence — Limburg.net / Kippenactie

## Message 1 — Initial outreach

**To:** info@limburg.net
**Subject:** Limburg.net's Kippenactie documented on CommunityFix — happy to get your input

---

Dear Limburg.net team,

I'm writing to let you know that your Kippenactie programme — the province-scale backyard-hen campaign distributed through a voucher and loyalty-points retail model — is now documented as a reference case study on CommunityFix (communityfix.org).

CommunityFix is an open platform that maps real-world solutions to environmental and community challenges so that other municipalities, waste authorities, and citizens worldwide can learn from them and replicate what works. Your programme is one of the most concrete examples we have found of a retail-partnered approach to reducing food waste at the household level.

The idea behind CommunityFix is simple: by documenting what was actually tried — and what came of it, costs and pitfalls included — other cities, regions and countries can avoid the same mistakes and go straight to the solutions that work. It's a small but real contribution to a better world: turning one community's hard-won experience into knowledge everyone can build on.

I'd like to ask two things:

1. **Accuracy check** — could someone on your team take a quick look at the case study and flag anything that needs correcting?
2. **Enrichment** — if you have recent participation figures, updated outcome data, lessons learned, or photos you are happy to share publicly, we would love to add them.

The case study is live at: https://communityfix.org

Please feel free to reply to this email with any feedback, or just point me to the right person and I'll follow up with them directly.

Thank you for the work you do — programmes like Kippenactie are exactly the kind of practical, scalable approach that other regions need to discover.

Warm regards,

Arnaud Gissinger
Founder — CommunityFix · communityfix.org
