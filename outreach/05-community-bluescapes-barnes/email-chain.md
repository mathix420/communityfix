## Message 1 — Initial outreach

**To:** communitybluescapes@richmond.gov.uk
**Subject:** Your Barnes High Street rain garden is a reference case study on CommunityFix

Hi,

I'm Arnaud, founder of CommunityFix (communityfix.org) — an open platform that documents real-world solutions to urban and community challenges so others can replicate them.

I wanted to let you know that the Community BlueScapes rain garden on Barnes High Street is now featured as a reference case study on the platform. The project stands out as a strong example of a council–NGO–resident partnership delivering retrofittable blue-green infrastructure on a busy high street — exactly the kind of practical, place-based approach that other communities are looking for.

I have two small asks:

1. **Accuracy check** — could you take a few minutes to read through the case study and flag anything that needs correcting? The link is: https://communityfix.org (case study #5 under the relevant solution node).
2. **Enrichment** — if you have updated figures (e.g. water volumes managed, resident engagement numbers), lessons learned, or photos you're happy to share under a Creative Commons licence, adding them would make the case study far more useful to practitioners trying to replicate the model.

The idea behind CommunityFix is simple: by documenting what was actually tried — and what came of it, costs and pitfalls included — other cities, regions and countries can avoid the same mistakes and go straight to the solutions that work. It's a small but real contribution to a better world: turning one community's hard-won experience into knowledge everyone can build on.

There's no commercial angle here — CommunityFix is a public resource and all content is freely accessible.

Happy to jump on a quick call if that's easier, or answer any questions by email.

Thank you for the work your partnership has done in Barnes.

Arnaud Gissinger
Founder — CommunityFix · communityfix.org
