Hello,

My name is Arnaud Gissinger and I'm the founder of CommunityFix (communityfix.org), an open knowledge platform where communities around the world share proven solutions to local challenges — from energy resilience to climate adaptation.

We've documented the City of Minneapolis's solar-plus-storage resilience hub programme — specifically the pilot installations in three disadvantaged communities designed to keep power, heating, and cooling available during outages — as a reference case study on our platform. It's a project we think many other cities will want to learn from and replicate.

I'm reaching out for two reasons:

1. **Accuracy review** — We want to make sure the information we've published reflects the programme as it actually stands. If anything is outdated or imprecise, we'd love to correct it.
2. **Enrichment** — If you're able to share updated figures (households served, outage hours covered, etc.), lessons learned, or photos from the installations, we can add them to the case study so other cities can learn from your experience as directly as possible.

The idea behind CommunityFix is simple: by documenting what was actually tried — and what came of it, costs and pitfalls included — other cities, regions and countries can avoid the same mistakes and go straight to the solutions that work. It's a small but real contribution to a better world: turning one community's hard-won experience into knowledge everyone can build on.

There's no commercial angle here — CommunityFix is a public resource, and all case studies are freely accessible.

Would someone on your team have 15–20 minutes to take a look and point out anything that needs updating?

Thank you for the work you're doing on energy equity in Minneapolis.

Warm regards,

Arnaud Gissinger
