Hi Jane,

I hope this finds you well. My name is Arnaud Gissinger — I'm the founder of CommunityFix (communityfix.org), an open platform that maps real-world responses to pressing community challenges so cities and organisations anywhere can learn from what's already working.

Miami-Dade's appointment of the world's first Chief Heat Officer is exactly the kind of pioneering, replicable intervention our community looks for. We've documented it as a reference case study on the platform, highlighting how the Arsht-Rock Resilience Center seeded the role and how it has evolved into a model that cities from Athens to Freetown are now adapting through the One Billion Resilient initiative.

I'm writing for two reasons:

1. **Accuracy** — We'd love for you or a colleague to cast an eye over the entry and flag anything that needs correcting or updating.

The entry is live and public; I'm happy to share the direct link if helpful.

The idea behind CommunityFix is simple: by documenting what was actually tried — and what came of it, costs and pitfalls included — other cities, regions and countries can avoid the same mistakes and go straight to the solutions that work. It's a small but real contribution to a better world: turning one community's hard-won experience into knowledge everyone can build on.
