Subject: CommunityFix — Your research on the 1872 Las Salinas solar desalination plant

Dear Dr. Arellano Escudero,

My name is Arnaud Gissinger, and I am the founder of CommunityFix (communityfix.org), an open platform that documents real-world solutions to community and sustainability challenges — from historical precedents to current initiatives.

I came across your work on the history of solar desalination, and I wanted to reach out because we have documented the 1872 Las Salinas solar still in the Atacama Desert as one of our case studies. Built by engineer Charles (Carlos) Wilson, it stands as a remarkable early example of solar technology applied at scale, and we believe it deserves wider visibility in contemporary sustainability discussions.

Given your expertise on this subject, I would be honoured if you were willing to review the case study and let us know whether the information we have is accurate, whether anything important is missing, or whether you would like to add context from your own research. Any corrections, sources, or insights you could share would be genuinely valuable.

The idea behind CommunityFix is simple: by documenting what was actually tried — and what came of it, costs and pitfalls included — other cities, regions and countries can avoid the same mistakes and go straight to the solutions that work. It's a small but real contribution to a better world: turning one community's hard-won experience into knowledge everyone can build on.

There is no obligation of any kind — even a brief note pointing us to a relevant publication would be welcome.

The platform is free, non-commercial, and open to anyone. You are welcome to browse it at communityfix.org.

Thank you for your time, and for the scholarly work you have done on this fascinating piece of history.
